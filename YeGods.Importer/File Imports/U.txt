A stone giant, brother to the weather and storm god TESHUB and to ARANZAH, personification of the river Tigris. All three are sons of KUMARBIS. He is blind, deaf and dumb as well as immobile, growing from mountain god UBELLURIS' shoulder. He grew until he touched heaven and disturbed the gods, who sent ISHTAR to dance naked for him to distract him. This didn't work, because Ullikummi could neither see nor hear her. Various attempts by the gods to destroy him failed, until EA called for the copper knife that had been used to separate heaven from earth. He severed Ullikummi from Ubelluris at the feet, and Teshub was then able to destroy him. Twin dormant volcanoes in Asia Minor bear Ullikummi's name, suggesting his legendary potential for causing widespread destruction

URANUS
Origin: Greek
Sex: M
Category: Deity
Aliases: URANOS
Though we owe Hesiod’s Theogony for much of the Uranus story, there are indications the myth is pre-Greek, possibly Hittite or even Oriental. It resembles the story of the Hittite KUNARBI; and the use of a scimitar indicates oriental sources. Uranus is the personification of heaven, and is the son of GAEA (Earth) Gaea unites with URANUS to produce the TITANS, CYCLOPES and the Hecatoncheires. Uranus is also father of CRONUS and RHEA. Uranus hated his offspring and hid them in Gaea’s body. Gaea persuaded CRONUS, a Titan, to avenge this, and when Uranus approached Gaea, Cronus lopped off Uranus’s testicles with a scimitar. The genitals floated on the sea producing a white foam from which sprang APHRODITE, goddess of love. Uranus’s consorts included HESTIA, NYX, HEMERA and CLYMENE

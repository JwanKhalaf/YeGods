FUKUROKUJO
Origin: Japan: Shintoism
Sex: M
Category: Deity
Aliases: JURŌJIN
One of the Seven Lucky Gods of Japanese mythology, and thought to have Chinese origins, since he looks like the Chinese star god SHOU, bald, with an abnormally high forehead, long whiskers and usually two symbols of longevity, a crane and a turtle. He could in fact be the Chinese Three Star Gods (FULUSHOU) as one deity. Legends suggest he started out as a human philosopher-hermit by the name of JURŌJIN, capable of performing miracles, and becoming thought of as a reincarnation of the Taoist deity XUANWU. He was said to have the celestial powers of the southern pole star. Later stories said he was Jurōjin's grandfather, or that Jurōjin inhabited the same body as Fukurokuju. His position as one of the Seven Lucky Gods was not always secure: in early representations, KICHIJŌTEN, the goddess of fortune, beauty and merit, is in his place. He is the god of longevity and wisdom, and is said to be the only god of the Seven capable of bringing the dead back to life
